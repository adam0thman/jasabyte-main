Service Delivery Guideline and Chargeability Policy

Professional Services

Professional services might be delivered in-person (on-site) or remotely. jasabyte policy on both delivery methods are the same:

One normal working man-day equates to eight (8) hour of service delivery - continuous or accumulated.
A normal working hours are defined as starting from 07:30AM to 07:30PM.
One chargeable block are measured in 30-minutes block.
Work executed prior or beyond the normal working hours are calculated with a 25% surcharge.
Work executed over weekend are calculated with 50% surcharge.
"Weekend" period are defined as starting from 00:01 of Saturday, to 23:59 of Sunday.

Attendance and Timesheets

Jasabyte encourages it's professionals to submit timesheet and/or attendance and/or other required documentation (such as Service Entry Sheet, system logon logs, et cetera) on weekly basis.

Documents can be submitted either on the Next Monday (or next working day of the next week), or the last working day of the week.
Last working dday of the week is defined as he last day starting from Monday to Sunday.
Document submission are generally be via email or any agreedd online communicatiton channel, but may be turned into physical copy upon final aporoval, where required.

Document Deliverables

Jasabyte try to, as much as possible complements it's clients' documentation change control and formatting, where possible, but has also adopted our own standards and guidelines.

Documents delivered will primarily be in Portable Document Format (PDF), unless explicitly agree for any specific project.
Documents will be having a change control formatting mechanism for file naming which include document versioniing, document creator initials as well as date of change. The document naming guideline is one of Jasabyte's standard project on-boarding templates that will be distributed toa ll project members, upon project initiation.
Document deliverable will and must always have a minimum of two verifier - one from Jasabyte's project lead and another from appointed counterpart from client side - before it can be deemed final.
